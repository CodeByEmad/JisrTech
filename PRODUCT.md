# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners and managers of traditional Saudi businesses (clinics, restaurants, retail, field-service firms) in the Eastern Province, plus procurement contacts at government agencies and larger organizations. Most browse on a phone, in Arabic first. They are being told everywhere to "go digital" and are wary of agencies that overpromise; they decide on trust, not feature lists. The job to be done: find one accountable partner who will build and keep running the software their business depends on.

## Product Purpose

The company-profile website of Jisr Tech (جسر تك), a software studio in Al Khobar. The site's single conversion goal is a WhatsApp conversation (contact form as the formal channel for organizations). It must demonstrate, by its own craft, the standard the studio sells: native bilingual Arabic/English, fast, honest. Success = qualified WhatsApp conversations started.

## Brand Personality

Enterprise-warm, confident, honest. "Jisr" means bridge; the brand is the crossing between a paper-and-phone-calls business and the digital world. Voice never begs, never hypes: it states what it does and shows the standard. Emotions to evoke: safety, competence, being taken seriously.

## Anti-references

- Startup-cute SaaS templates: purple gradients, floating 3D blobs, emoji, playful mascots.
- Agency bravado: Awwwards experimentation, scroll hijacking, huge counters of invented stats.
- Legalistic corporate: stock photos of handshakes, contract-speak, ticket-system coldness.
- Anything that fakes proof: client logos, testimonials, ratings, team photos are all banned until real (company policy: total honesty, no founder identity, no prices on the site).

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The site is the portfolio. Every detail (Arabic typography, RTL mirroring, speed) is evidence of the service quality.
2. **Honesty is structural.** Only verifiable facts render; evidence slots stay empty until real evidence exists.
3. **One path across.** Every section moves the visitor toward one WhatsApp conversation; no competing CTAs, no prices, no detours.
4. **Arabic is the original.** Both languages are written and art-directed natively; the Arabic page must never feel like a mirrored translation.
5. **Warm authority.** Premium restraint over decoration; craft signals (type, rhythm, motion) rather than louder marketing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast minimum, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere (motion collapses to visible static states), semantic HTML, skip link, correct `lang`/`dir` per locale. Bilingual parity is structural: content that misses a locale cannot compile.
